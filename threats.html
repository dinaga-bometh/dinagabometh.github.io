Threats to Turtles

Turtles face numerous threats to their survival due to human activities and natural causes. Understanding these threats is crucial for their conservation. Some common threats include:

Pollution

Pollution, especially marine pollution caused by plastics and chemicals, poses a significant threat to turtles. They can mistake plastic debris for food or get entangled in discarded fishing nets.

Habitat Loss

Loss and degradation of nesting beaches, coral reefs, seagrass beds, and other essential habitats threaten turtles' ability to find suitable nesting sites, forage for food, and complete their life cycles.

Illegal Trade

Turtles and their eggs are often illegally collected and traded for their meat, shells, and as exotic pets. This trade puts additional pressure on already vulnerable turtle populations.

Climate Change

The rise in sea levels, changes in temperature, and altered weather patterns associated with climate change affect turtle nesting beaches, disrupt their food sources, and impact their overall survival.
